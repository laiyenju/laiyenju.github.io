## Projection Introduction

This is a team workshop project guided by [Gary Hsieh](https://www.hcde.washington.edu/hsieh). I'm one of the 4 team-members in this project. I participated in idea iterations through all steps, been in charge of dessigning the final prototype.


| Designing Challenge            | Client                                 | Deliverable                                                                                               |
| ------------------------------ | -------------------------------------- | --------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------------- |
| reading diverse political news | [UDN news](https://udn.com/news/index) | New layout for the front page and/or article pages. Concept creation of additional experience is welcome. |


## Persona

Regi is a firsr-generation college student who wants to make their family proud. For Regi, debating and finding common ground is a life calling. He wants to get into politics, so it is key to stay updated with current events.

more about Regi:

- is generally a calm person
- works part-time
- self-supported

## Scenario

As Regi heads home after a day of classes, they think it is a good idea to read the news. **The UDN website is bookmarked on their browser**, and Regi clicks on the link to visit the site. He **opens up 5 articles aligning with his political views**, but after reading 2 of them, decides it is better to take a shower and proceed to study for next week's Art History exam. Regi closes the **laptop**.


## Sprint Steps

1. Translate scenario into user journey
2. Apply strategies to user journey
3. Scope work
4. Sketch ideas
5. Select an idea
6. Prototype

### Step 2: Apply Strategies to User Journey

Goal setting theory and social engagement are our references. We focus on following points to build up strategies.

- The establishment of specific, difficult goals will lead to a higher performance and motivation users in a participatory creation process.
- People are more likely to achieve their goal if it is broken down into smaller chunks.

We decide to form a readers' chat group. In this group, every reader could join sub-group, with different political ideologies, to dicuss specific political issues based on UDN's news articles.
